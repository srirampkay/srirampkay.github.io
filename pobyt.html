Overview

Pobyt is a tech startup based in India that allows hotel reservation for short stays. I worked as a Product Designer with Pobyt for over 2 years. When I took charge, Pobyt was plagued with inconsistent ux patterns, poor visuals and too much information that caused confusion for its users. Below is a case study that explains the redesign process.

Problem Overview

During the initial stages, Pobyt started with a very small team and hence had no budget to invest in a team to focus on the user experience and the visual aspects of the product. When the idea was picthed, it seemed very simple. It is like every other room booking app in the market. But, it was plagued with inconsistent ux patterns, poor visuals and too much information that caused confusion for its users.

Problem Summary

1. User Expectations

The idea of "Pay Per Minute" sounds genuine and viable. This creates a lot of anticipation and expectations on Pobyt. Slowness and lack of clear visuals cause confusion.

2. Unclear User flow

This was because users were unaware how to navigate through screens.

3. Lack of Transparency

Pobyt calculates the tax component only in the later screens. This makes users feel "There is inconsistent pricing across pages"

4. Cognitive Overload

Pobyt tries to communicate a ton of information all the time. This increases the processing time thereby creating cognitive overload.

5. Inconsistency

Visuals and UX patterns are not consistent across the app. This could create a lot of confusion and sometimes frustration in users.
