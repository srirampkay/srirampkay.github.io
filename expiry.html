Overview

Expiry is a new food delivery service that only delivers food from grocery stores and restaurants that are just meeting the end of their shelf life – think that sushi box from Whole Foods or this morning’s pastries from the local bakery.

View Case Study

Final design

Problem Statement

Expiry has been operating under a mystery box monthly subscription that lets users set their preferences (e.g. number of people, vegan, only produce) and allows them to place an order before noon for evening delivery.

Recently, Expiry has been losing customers. After doing user research, it turned out the customers want more control over their service.

My task was to design an experience that allows customers to easily place same-day orders from a dynamic, limited supply of food.

Assumptions/Rules


Existing problems

So, Customers feel they don't have enough control over their orders. This could mean:

1. Customers are unhappy with the food they receive

Existing design allows users to set preferences like Vegan or Vegetarian. This could result in customers getting food they may not like.
Example 1. Customer who doesn't like Sushi, gets Sushi in their mystery box.
Example 2. Customer gets food with ingredents they are allergic to(Peanut allergy).

2. Customers are unaware of their order status

Existing design doesn't let users track their food. This could result in food left at the user's doorstep. If not notified, it could remain there for hours.

3. "Change of plans" for the customer

Due to unforseen reasons, if the customer needs to change his plans for the evening. The current design doesn't let subscribers "skip" delivery for that day. This could result in more food wastage and loss for the subscriber.
Example: The customer gets invited to a birthday party for the evening and he/she needs to cancel delivery for that day
